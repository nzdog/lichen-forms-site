Lichen Protocol — Intake Questionnaire

A field is the environment you're building inside — the expectations, pressures, norms, and invisible forces that shape how you work and make decisions. Every founder operates inside a field, whether they realise it or not.

This surfaces the baseline you're operating inside — what you've come to accept as "just how things are." It helps the system understand the default conditions shaping you before any shift happens.

Examples
  • "Constant context switching, lots of pings, and never quite finishing anything."
  • "Back-to-back calls, always a bit behind, but on the surface everything looks fine."
  • "Mostly solo deep focus, but with a constant low-level worry about sustainability."

This reveals the behaviours your field currently reinforces — what gets praise, traction, or survival. It shows what you're being conditioned to optimise for.

Examples
  • "Shipping fast even if quality drops."
  • "Being available 24/7 and responding instantly."
  • "Keeping investors calm instead of telling the truth."
  • "Cleaning up other people's messes instead of building what actually matters to me."

This shows how your field responds under stress — whether it supports you, amplifies pressure, or leaves you alone. It reveals the resilience or brittleness of your current conditions.

Examples
  • "I isolate and push harder; no one really knows when I'm struggling."
  • "The team is supportive, but I still feel like I can't show doubt."
  • "Everyone panics and blames each other — full firefighting mode."
  • "We pause, talk openly, and re-scope together. Not perfect, but I don't feel alone."

This highlights how clearly you're thinking inside your current conditions — whether decisions come from coherence or from pressure, fear, or habit. It helps the system gauge your internal signal-to-noise ratio.

Examples
  • "Reactive; mostly putting out fires and saying yes to whatever is loudest."
  • "Strategic in theory, but I override my instincts to keep people happy."
  • "Good when I give myself time — I just rarely take that time."
  • "Sharp on product decisions, avoidant on people decisions."

This draws out the real price you're paying — energetically, relationally, financially — for keeping conditions as they are. It shows where the trajectory is unsustainable.

Examples
  • "My health is taking the hit — poor sleep, constant anxiety."
  • "My partner is losing patience; I'm never fully present."
  • "I'm burning savings with no clear sustainability path."
  • "I'm losing connection to why I started this."

This points toward the conditions under which you could actually thrive. It gives the system direction, not just a list of stresses.

Examples
  • "Calm, focused conditions where I can think clearly and produce meaningful work."
  • "An field where honesty, nuance, and long-term thinking are normal."
  • "A rhythm where I can be present with family and still build ambitiously."
  • "One that rewards craftsmanship, coherence, and alignment — not nonstop pressure."
