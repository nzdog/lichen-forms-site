7-day check-in — lichen protocol

A reflection on the week since your session to track how the field is settling.

This reveals which parts of your recent sessions have created durable movement. After a week — regardless of how many protocols you've walked — the noise settles and what remains is genuine integration, not just immediate reaction.

Examples
  • "The calm hasn't faded. Decisions are still slower and more intentional."
  • "The clarity about my next step has stayed consistent all week."
  • "The sense of pressure is still lower — not gone, but noticeably lighter."
  • "My focus has remained sharper; I'm not getting derailed as easily."

This shows where older patterns still have gravitational pull, and where environmental or internal pressures remain unchanged. It helps the system see which structures reassert themselves and which are ready to move.

Examples
  • "Some of the old urgency crept back mid-week."
  • "The team dynamic still feels chaotic — no change yet."
  • "The insight faded after two days; I slipped back into autopilot."
  • "Nothing external changed this week."

This indicates how your internal landscape has reorganised across the week. Clarity and possibility are reliable markers of deepening coherence, especially when multiple sessions contribute to cumulative movement.

Examples
  • "Delegating feels genuinely possible now."
  • "I can see the real issue behind the surface confusion."
  • "I feel permission to say no to things that aren't mine."
  • "The direction for the next month feels obvious instead of foggy."

This measures field-level movement — how you relate to the pressures, signals, and demands of your environment. Even if nothing external changes, your relationship to it can shift substantially across a week.

Examples
  • "Investor pressure feels less personal — more like neutral information."
  • "I'm less reactive to my inbox; I don't feel pulled around by it."
  • "The team's urgency doesn't hijack me anymore."
  • "Nothing external has shifted, but I feel more grounded inside it."

Somatic stability is one of the clearest indicators of real coherence. Across a week, your body reveals whether shifts are integrating, holding steady, or returning to old patterns.

Examples
  • "My shoulders have stayed more relaxed all week."
  • "Tightness comes back when talking to a particular stakeholder."
  • "My breathing has been slower and more even."
  • "No major changes, but I'm more conscious of when my chest tightens."
